🦮 Functions of the Skeleton ▼

🛠 Support

Provides the structural framework that holds the body upright.

Eg: Vertebral column supports the head and trunk

🛡 Protection

Shields vital organs from injury.

Eg: Skull protects the brain; ribcage protects heart & lungs

⚡ Movement

Bones act as levers; muscles pull on bones to create movement.

Eg: Femur & tibia form the lever system at the knee

📅 Blood Cell Production

Red bone marrow in long bones produces red and white blood cells.

Eg: Femur, tibia, humerus

🧦 Mineral Storage

Stores calcium and phosphorus; released into the blood when needed.

Eg: Calcium stored in the cortical bone matrix

🏭 Energy Storage

Yellow marrow in long bones stores fat as an energy reserve.

Eg: Diaphysis (shaft) of the femur

💡 Exam tip: You must know ALL six functions. "Support, Protection, Movement, Blood cell production, Mineral storage, Energy storage" — memorise this list.

💡 For any movement in the exam, you should be able to identify the agonist and antagonist. Practice with: squat (agonist = quadriceps, antagonist = hamstrings), shoulder press, knee extension.

💡 Eccentric contractions cause the most DOMS (delayed onset muscle soreness) — a common exam link. Students often confuse eccentric with "relaxing" — it is still an ACTIVE contraction.

⚖️ Lever Systems in the Body ▼
ClassOrder (F-A-R)AdvantageBody ExampleSport Example
1st Class Force — Axis — Resistance (FAR or RAFboth sides) Can produce force OR speed depending on distances Neck extension: atlas (axis), head (resistance), posterior neck muscles (force) Heading a soccer ball
2nd Class Axis — Resistance — Force (ARF) Force advantage — can move heavy loads Plantarflexion: metatarsals (axis), body weight (resistance), gastrocnemius (force) Calf raise, rising onto toes
3rd Class Axis — Force — Resistance (AFR) Speed/range advantage — most common in body Elbow flexion: elbow (axis), biceps insertion (force), hand/forearm (resistance) Throwing, kicking, batting

💡 3rd class levers are the MOST COMMON in the body — sacrifice force for speed and range. 2nd class is RAREST. A seesaw is 1st class; a wheelbarrow is 2nd class — good memory anchors.
