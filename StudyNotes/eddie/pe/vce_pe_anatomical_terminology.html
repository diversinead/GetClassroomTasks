Term Category Opposite Meaning Body / sport example Plane Exam note
AnteriorDirectional Posterior Towards the front of the body Patella (kneecap) is on the anterior surface of the knee —Also called ventral
PosteriorDirectional Anterior Towards the back of the body Hamstrings are on the posterior surface of the thigh —Also called dorsal
SuperiorDirectional Inferior Above; higher toward the head The shoulder is superior to the elbow —Also called cranial
InferiorDirectional Superior Below; lower toward the feet The knee is inferior to the hip —Also called caudal
MedialDirectional Lateral Towards the midline of the body Big toe is medial; inner knee ligament (MCL) is medial —Inner side of a limb
LateralDirectional Medial Away from the midline of the body Little toe is lateral; outer knee ligament (LCL) is lateral —Outer side of a limb
ProximalDirectional Distal Closer to the trunk or point of attachment The knee is proximal to the ankle; the shoulder is proximal to the wrist —Limbs only
DistalDirectional Proximal Further from the trunk or point of attachment The ankle is distal to the knee; fingers are distal to the wrist —Limbs only
SuperficialDirectional Deep Close to the body's outer surface Skin is superficial to muscle; a graze is a superficial injury ——
DeepDirectional Superficial Further beneath the body's surface Bone is deep to muscle; a deep tissue injury affects inner muscle layers ——
Sagittal planePlane — Divides body into left and right halves; movements go forwards and backwards Bicep curl, squat, running stride, push-up SagittalAxis of rotation = mediolateral
Frontal planePlane — Divides body into front and back halves; movements go side to side Star jump, lateral raise, side bend, cartwheel FrontalAlso called coronal; axis = anteroposterior
Transverse planePlane — Divides body into top and bottom halves; rotational movements Trunk rotation, golf swing, pivot step in netball, discus throw TransverseAxis = longitudinal (vertical)
FlexionMovement Extension Decreasing the angle at a joint; bending Bending the elbow in a bicep curl; bending the knee when squatting SagittalOccurs at most joints
ExtensionMovement Flexion Increasing the angle at a joint; straightening Straightening the elbow at the top of a push-up; standing from a squat SagittalOccurs at most joints
HyperextensionMovement Flexion Extension beyond the neutral/anatomical position — past the straight joint angle Back bend in gymnastics; heading a soccer ball (neck); swan dive entry SagittalCan signal injury risk if forced
AbductionMovement Adduction Moving a limb away from the body's midline Raising the arm to the side (lateral raise); star jump out phase FrontalMemory: ABsent from midline
AdductionMovement Abduction Moving a limb toward the body's midline Bringing the arm back to the side; star jump return phase FrontalBall & socket and some condyloid joints
DorsiflexionMovement Plantarflexion Pulling the toes toward the shin — decreasing the angle at the top (dorsal) surface of the ankle Heel strike in running; walking up stairs; landing from a jump SagittalAnkle only — do not apply to other joints
PlantarflexionMovement Dorsiflexion Pointing the toes away from the shin — increasing the angle at the sole (plantar) surface of the ankle Push-off in sprinting; calf raise; en pointe in dance SagittalAnkle only — do not apply to other joints
RotationMovement — Turning a body segment around its long axis Trunk rotation in a golf swing; head turn; pivot step in netball TransverseMedial = inward; lateral = outward
CircumductionMovement — Circular movement combining flexion, extension, abduction and adduction — limb traces a cone Bowler's delivery in cricket; arm windmill; hip circle in gymnastics All planesBall & socket joints only (hip, shoulder). NOT possible at hinge joints.
