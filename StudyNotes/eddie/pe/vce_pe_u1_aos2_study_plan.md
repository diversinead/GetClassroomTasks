## Wednesday 13 May — Respiratory Structure & Breathing Mechanics
⏱ **Suggested time: 2 hours**

**Focus:** Checklist Chapter 4 (Respiratory) — sections 4.2 and 4.3

- [ ] **(30 min)** Read **Ch4a: The respiratory system** study notes. Focus on:
   - The 5 functions of the respiratory system
   - Path of air: nasal cavity → pharynx → larynx → trachea → bronchi → bronchioles → alveoli (memorise this in ORDER)
   - Features of alveoli that make them ideal for gas exchange (thin walls, large SA, capillary network)
- [ ] **(30 min)** Mechanics of breathing:
   - **Inspiration:** diaphragm contracts (down), intercostals contract (ribs up/out), chest expands, pressure drops, air rushes in
   - **Expiration:** diaphragm relaxes (up), ribs down/in, pressure increases, air pushed out
   - Memorise **V = TV × RR** and practise 2–3 calculations
- [ ] **(30 min)** Lung volumes — define and distinguish: tidal volume, vital capacity, residual volume, total lung capacity, inspiratory reserve volume, expiratory reserve volume.
- [ ] **(15 min)** Define **VO₂ max** — including why it's expressed as mL/kg/min.
- [ ] **(15 min)** Complete **Q4a: The respiratory system** quiz.
- [ ] **Tick on checklist:** all of Section 4.2 (5 items) and 4.3 (9 items)

## Saturday 16 May — Prohibited Substances + Ethics
⏱ **Suggested time: 2 hours**

**Focus:** Checklist Chapter 5 — sections 5.4 and 5.5

- [ ] **(40 min)** Read **Ch5b: Permitted & prohibited performance enhancement** (Prohibited half). Master each substance with this structure: **what it is → benefit → harms → why banned**:
   - **EPO** — kidney hormone, synthetic form ↑ RBCs, harms: blood clotting/stroke/heart attack
   - **Blood doping** — same effect as EPO; autologous (own blood) or homologous (donor)
   - **Beta blockers** — block adrenaline; ↓ HR, ↓ tremor; banned in precision sports (archery, shooting); harms: hypotension, asthma, cardiac failure
   - **Gene doping** — manipulating genes/cells for performance gain; still emerging
- [ ] **(20 min)** For each prohibited substance, identify the **permitted alternative**:
   - EPO / blood doping → aerobic OR altitude training
   - Beta blockers → psychological techniques (breathing, mental rehearsal)
- [ ] **(30 min)** Ethical & sociocultural considerations:
   - **Sociocultural factors** pressuring athletes: income, fame, coach influence, cultural norms, peer pressure
   - The **WADA 2-of-3 criteria**: enhances performance / health risk / violates spirit of sport
   - **Fair and level playing field** principle
- [ ] **(15 min)** Practise a 4–6 mark question: *"Is gene doping ethically acceptable? Justify."* Write a structured response.
- [ ] **(15 min)** Complete **Q5b: Permitted & prohibited performance enhancement** quiz.
- [ ] **Tick on checklist:** Section 5.4 (5 items) and 5.5 (4 items)

## Tips for Each Session

- **Don't just re-read** — the brain registers reading as "I know this" without actually retaining. Always test yourself with the quiz at the end of each session.
- **Draw the diagrams from memory** — heart chambers, respiratory tract, alveolar/capillary gas exchange. If you can draw + label them, you really know them.
- **Speak the answers out loud** — explain a concept to an empty room. If you stumble, you don't know it well enough.
- **Use the SVG diagrams in the study notes** — they're built for memorisation. Open them, study them, close them, redraw.
- **Sleep matters more than the last 30 minutes of study.** Don't sacrifice it.
