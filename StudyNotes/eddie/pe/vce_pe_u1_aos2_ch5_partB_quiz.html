Short Answer3 marks
1. State the three WADA criteria used to determine whether a substance or method should be prohibited. Explain how many of these criteria need to be met for prohibition.
✅ Model Answer
✓1. It has the potential to (or actually does) enhance performance in sport. (1 mark)
✓2. It has the potential or represents an actual risk to the athlete's health. (1 mark)
✓3. It violates the spirit of sport (as described in the WADA Code). A substance/method is prohibited when it meets at least TWO of these three criteria. (1 mark)
⚠ Examiner tip: The “at least 2 of 3” rule is a hot exam topic. Memorise the three criteria precisely.
Short Answer4 marks
2. Describe TWO ethical considerations AND TWO sociocultural considerations associated with the use of performance-enhancing strategies.
✅ Model Answer
✓Ethical 1 (any one of): Legal compliance - does the strategy comply with WADA/Sport Integrity Australia? OR Honesty - is there transparency in use? OR Fairness - is there a level playing field? (1 mark)
✓Ethical 2 (different from above): Safety - are there health risks? OR Role-modelling - does it reflect community values? (1 mark)
✓Sociocultural 1 (any one of): Cultural norms - what does society consider fair play? OR Peers - unspoken pressures within the team? OR Social acceptance - is doping normalised in this sport? (1 mark)
✓Sociocultural 2 (different from above): Socioeconomic status - does income affect choices? OR Education - have athletes been informed about risks? (1 mark)
⚠ Examiner tip: Each consideration should be NAMED then BRIEFLY EXPLAINED - one without the other won't earn full marks. Don't repeat themes from the same category.
Short Answer4 marks
3. Describe how altitude training improves endurance performance. Name THREE specific physiological adaptations that occur.
✅ Model Answer
✓Altitude training exposes the body to a hypoxic (low-oxygen) environment, typically at 2000+ metres elevation. This forces the body to adapt to cope with reduced O2 availability. (1 mark)
✓Adaptation 1: Elevated EPO production by the kidneys triggers greater production of red blood cells, enhancing the blood's O2-carrying capacity. (1 mark)
✓Adaptation 2: Increased alveolar size, number and surface area for greater gaseous exchange at the lungs. (1 mark)
✓Adaptation 3 (any): Increased capillarisation for better O2 delivery to muscles AND/OR enhanced mitochondrial production for more efficient aerobic ATP production. (1 mark)
⚠ Examiner tip: Always name the hypoxic environment as the trigger. Each named adaptation must include WHY it improves performance - just listing them won't score full marks.
Short Answer4 marks
An endurance cyclist is considering using EPO to enhance their performance in an upcoming race.
4. Explain how EPO would improve performance, AND describe TWO potential health risks of EPO use.
✅ Model Answer
✓Synthetic EPO injection significantly increases red blood cell production above normal levels. More RBCs means much greater oxygen-carrying capacity. (1 mark)
✓More oxygen reaching working muscles = higher VO2 max, greater aerobic energy production, improved endurance performance and faster recovery. (1 mark)
✓Risk 1: Blood clotting - the blood becomes thicker and more viscous, increasing the risk of blood clots, strokes or heart attacks. (1 mark)
✓Risk 2 (any other): High blood pressure leading to long-term vessel damage; heart/kidney damage from working harder; potentially fatal cardiovascular events. (1 mark)
⚠ Examiner tip: Always link the BENEFIT to physiology (more RBCs → more O2 → better aerobic energy). For risks, be specific - “harmful to health” isn't enough.
Extended Response6 marks
A national-level archer is preparing for the Olympics. Their coach mentions that beta blockers could help with their performance, but they are concerned about the legality and consequences.
5. (a) Explain what beta blockers are and HOW they work. (b) Explain why beta blockers are permitted in many sports but PROHIBITED in archery. (c) Identify TWO ethical considerations the athlete should reflect on, AND TWO health risks they should be aware of.
✅ Model Answer
✓(a) Beta blockers are medications that work by blocking the effects of adrenaline, resulting in reduced heart rate and widened blood vessels. (1 mark)
✓(b) In most sports, beta blockers don't enhance endurance, strength or speed - they actually reduce performance - so they are permitted. (1 mark)
✓(b cont.) In archery (and other precision sports like pistol shooting), they provide an unfair advantage by decreasing tremor (steadier hand) and reducing anxiety (optimal arousal) - so they are prohibited. (1 mark)
✓(c) Ethical 1: Legal compliance - using a banned substance in archery violates WADA/Sport Integrity Australia rules and would result in sanctions. (1 mark)
✓(c cont.) Ethical 2: Fairness - violates the “level playing field” principle by gaining an advantage other athletes choose not to take. OR Role-modelling - high-profile athletes set examples for younger competitors. (1 mark)
✓(c cont.) Two health risks (any two): hypotension (low blood pressure); altered blood sugar; asthma attacks; cardiac failure. (1 mark)
⚠ Examiner tip: Mechanism FIRST (blocks adrenaline → reduces HR & tremor), then SPORT-SPECIFIC explanation of why it's banned in precision sports. Tie ethics back to WADA and fairness.
