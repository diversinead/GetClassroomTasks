📊 Reference Tables

🦮 Functions of the Skeleton ▼

🛠 Support

Provides structural framework holding the body upright.

Vertebral column supports head and trunk

🛡 Protection

Shields vital organs from injury.

Skull protects brain; ribcage protects heart and lungs

⚡ Movement

Bones act as levers; muscles pull on them to create movement.

Femur and tibia form the lever system at the knee

📅 Blood Cell Production

Red bone marrow produces red and white blood cells.

Femur, tibia, humerus

🧦 Mineral Storage

Stores calcium and phosphorus; released when needed.

Calcium stored in cortical bone matrix

🏭 Energy Storage

Yellow marrow stores fat as an energy reserve.

Diaphysis (shaft) of the femur

💡 You must know ALL six functions. Memorise: Support, Protection, Movement, Blood cell production, Mineral storage, Energy storage.
