📋 Short Answer Questions
Short Answer3 marks
David Foss owns Dynamic Gym in Essendon. From his records for the year ended 31 August 2028 he has provided the following data (extract):
Capital$20,000
Loan from bank$10,000
Drawings$35,000
Casual wages$18,800
Laptop computer purchased$2,600
Rent$4,100
Interest on loans$1,200
Sales$95,400
List THREE items from David's data that should NOT appear on the income statement, and give a brief reason for each. (3 marks)
✅ Model Answer

Any three of the following, each correctly identified with a reason (1 mark each):

• Capital ($20,000): an owner's equity contribution, recorded on the balance sheet — not revenue.

• Loan from bank ($10,000): a liability the business must repay, recorded on the balance sheet — not revenue.

• Drawings ($35,000): an owner's equity withdrawal that reduces capital, not an expense.

• Laptop computer ($2,600): a non-current asset, recorded on the balance sheet — not an expense for the period.

⚠ Listing the items alone earns half marks. The question asks for a brief reason — an answer of "Capital, Loan, Drawings" with no justification typically scores 1.5/3, not 3/3.
Short Answer★ HOT4 marks
Warrnambool Trailer Hire reports the following for the year:
Sales revenue$125,000
Trailer repairs$24,250
Wages$61,250
Office expenses$12,000
a. Calculate the net profit margin. (2 marks)
b. The owner says: "I'm happy — I made $27,500 profit." Using your NPM, comment on whether this view is sufficient. (2 marks)
✅ Model Answer

(a) NPM (2 marks): Net profit = $125,000 − $24,250 − $61,250 − $12,000 = $27,500 (1 mark). NPM = $27,500 ÷ $125,000 × 100 = 22% (1 mark).

(b) Comment (2 marks):

• The owner is right that $27,500 is a positive dollar profit (1 mark).

• However, the 22% NPM means 78c of every sales dollar is consumed by expenses. To know whether 22% is healthy, the owner must compare it to a benchmark — prior year, industry average or budget. Without that comparison, the dollar profit on its own does not reveal whether margin is trending up or down (1 mark).

💡 "Compare to a benchmark" is the key phrase examiners are looking for — an answer that just quotes the 22% without explaining it needs context will be capped at 1/2 on the comment part.
Short Answer★ HOT4 marks
Bill's Mowing and Lucy's Lawn Services have reported the following information:
ItemBill's MowingLucy's Lawn Services
Current assets$24,000$38,000
Current liabilities$16,000$29,000
a. Calculate the working capital ratio for each business. (2 marks)
b. Which business has the better liquidity, and why? (2 marks)
✅ Model Answer

(a) Working capital ratios (2 marks):

• Bill's: $24,000 ÷ $16,000 = 1.5 : 1 (1 mark)

• Lucy's: $38,000 ÷ $29,000 = 1.31 : 1 (1 mark)

(b) Better liquidity (2 marks):

• Bill's Mowing has the better liquidity at 1.5 : 1 vs Lucy's 1.31 : 1 (1 mark).

• Bill's holds $1.50 of current assets for every $1 of current liabilities, whereas Lucy's holds only $1.31 — so Bill's has more headroom to meet short-term obligations. The higher dollar amount of current assets at Lucy's ($38k vs $24k) is irrelevant because liabilities are also proportionately higher (1 mark).

⚠ A common error is to assume Lucy's is better because her current assets are bigger in dollar terms. The RATIO is what measures liquidity — absolute dollar size doesn't.
📚 Extended Response Questions
Extended Response★ HOT8 marks
The owner of Bendigo Tractor Repairs has provided the following information about her business. She is pleased because net profit has risen each year.
Item31 Dec 202531 Dec 202631 Dec 202731 Dec 2028
Net profit$80,000$90,000$110,000$120,000
Owner's capital$400,000$400,000$450,000$460,000
Total assets$600,000$620,000$700,000$800,000
a. Calculate the return on owner's investment (ROI) and rate of return on assets (ROA) for the years ended 31 December 2026, 2027 and 2028. Use average capital and average total assets. (4 marks)
b. The owner is pleased that profit has risen every year. Using your calculations, comment on whether the trend supports her satisfaction. (3 marks)
c. Identify one additional financial indicator the owner could use to evaluate her business, with a brief justification. (1 mark)
✅ Model Answer

(a) ROI and ROA (4 marks — 0.5 each):

• 2026 ROI = $90,000 ÷ avg($400k, $400k) = 90,000 ÷ 400,000 = 22.5%

• 2026 ROA = $90,000 ÷ avg($600k, $620k) = 90,000 ÷ 610,000 = 14.75%

• 2027 ROI = $110,000 ÷ avg($400k, $450k) = 110,000 ÷ 425,000 = 25.88%

• 2027 ROA = $110,000 ÷ avg($620k, $700k) = 110,000 ÷ 660,000 = 16.67%

• 2028 ROI = $120,000 ÷ avg($450k, $460k) = 120,000 ÷ 455,000 = 26.37%

• 2028 ROA = $120,000 ÷ avg($700k, $800k) = 120,000 ÷ 750,000 = 16.00%

(b) Comment on trend (3 marks):

• The owner is correct that net profit AND ROI have both risen every year — each dollar of her capital is earning a higher return, so she is being better rewarded for her investment (1 mark).

• However ROA rose from 14.75% to 16.67% between 2026 and 2027, then FELL to 16.00% in 2028 — the business is becoming less efficient at using its assets to generate profit (1 mark).

• The 2028 dip is explained by total assets jumping $100k (from $700k to $800k) while profit only rose $10k. The new assets are not yet generating proportional returns. Her satisfaction with rising dollar profit is therefore incomplete — profit must keep pace with the growing asset base for the business to be improving (1 mark).

(c) Additional indicator (1 mark):

• Working capital ratio — to check whether the business can meet its short-term debts (the indicators above all measure long-term profitability, not liquidity). OR Debt ratio — to see whether the growing asset base has been funded by debt, increasing financial risk.

💡 The trick to this question is spotting that the ASSET base grew faster than profit. Students who notice only the rising ROI and miss the ROA fall typically cap at 5/8.
Extended Response★ HOT8 marks
The owner of Carnegie Car Wash has calculated the following debt ratios for her business. She is happy with performance because profit has been steady over the past few years.
Year202620272028
Debt ratio45%67%75%
a. Explain what the trend in the debt ratio indicates to the owner. (2 marks)
b. Describe one ADVANTAGE for a small business of increasing the debt ratio. (2 marks)
c. Describe one DISADVANTAGE of an increasing debt ratio. (2 marks)
d. Despite steady profit, explain why the trend in the debt ratio is a concern. (2 marks)
✅ Model Answer

(a) Trend (2 marks):

• The debt ratio has risen sharply from 45% to 75% over three years — a 30-percentage-point increase (1 mark).

• 75c of every $1 of assets is now funded by debt rather than the owner. The business is becoming increasingly geared and financially fragile (1 mark).

(b) Advantage (2 marks):

• Higher debt allows the business to acquire more assets and grow faster than the owner could fund alone (1 mark).

• In profitable years, borrowed funds amplify the return on the owner's capital — leverage boosts ROI (positive gearing) (1 mark).

(c) Disadvantage (2 marks):

• Interest expense is fixed regardless of trading conditions, so high gearing magnifies losses if profit falls (1 mark).

• The business may struggle to obtain further finance — lenders see the existing debt load and limited asset buffer (1 mark).

(d) Why the trend is a concern (2 marks):

• Although profit is steady today, the business now relies heavily on debt — a small rise in interest rates or a profit dip could leave Carnegie unable to meet interest commitments (1 mark).

• At 75% debt ratio, there is little asset buffer remaining; banks may refuse further borrowing, leaving the business unable to fund any unexpected shortfall — a stable-looking present masks a fragile future (1 mark).

⚠ "Steady profit" tempts students to defend the owner's view. The question is testing whether you can argue against the surface signal using the trend.
Extended Response8 marks
A restaurant owner has calculated her financial indicators for the year: net profit margin 15%, ROI 12%, working capital ratio 1.8 : 1, debt ratio 52%. She asks for help interpreting them and is open to additional ways to evaluate the business.
a. Explain why benchmarks are important when evaluating profit performance, and identify THREE suitable benchmarks for the restaurant owner. (3 marks)
b. State and describe TWO non-financial indicators the restaurant owner could use to evaluate business performance. (4 marks)
c. Explain how a non-financial indicator can warn the owner of a problem BEFORE it shows in the financial reports. (1 mark)
✅ Model Answer

(a) Benchmarks (3 marks):

• Indicators only have meaning in COMPARISON — a 15% NPM is good or bad depending on context. Benchmarks supply that context (1 mark).

• Three suitable benchmarks for the restaurant: (i) the restaurant's own PRIOR YEARS (trend over time); (ii) the INDUSTRY AVERAGE for restaurants of similar size; (iii) the owner's BUDGET or TARGETS for the year (2 marks — any 3 valid, with brief justification).

(b) Two non-financial indicators (4 marks):

• Customer satisfaction (1 mark) — measured by review scores, repeat-customer %, or net promoter score. Falling satisfaction predicts falling revenue 1–2 quarters later, so tracking it lets the owner act before sales drop (1 mark).

• Staff turnover (1 mark) — the % of staff leaving per year. High turnover costs recruitment and training, disrupts service quality, and often signals deeper culture problems that erode performance (1 mark).

(Other acceptable indicators: time to deliver service, food waste %, environmental performance, market share.)

(c) Leading indicator (1 mark):

• A drop in customer satisfaction this month typically shows up as falling revenue several months later. By the time the income statement reflects the damage, it is too late to prevent. Tracking customer feedback gives the owner an earlier signal and time to act on service quality (1 mark).

💡 For full marks on (b), pick indicators from DIFFERENT categories — one customer-related, one staff/ops-related. Two customer indicators in the same answer often cost 1 mark for lacking breadth.
