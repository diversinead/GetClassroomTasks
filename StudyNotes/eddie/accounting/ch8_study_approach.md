# Ch 8 Study Approach — The 4 Questions to Focus On

*Yr 11 Accounting — Income Statements for a Service Firm*

Rather than half-doing seven exercises, do these four properly. Together they cover every skill the chapter teaches.

---

## The 4 Questions

### 1. Q1 — Learn to Play Guitar *(the warm-up)*
**Time:** ~30 min
**Role:** Build confidence with the cleanest possible example. No tricky transactions, no mid-period capital contribution — just a clean conversion from Statement of Receipts and Payments to Income Statement.

### 2. Q3 — Cavalieri's Computer Services *(the comprehensive one)*
**Time:** ~30 min review
**Role:** Reinforces Q1 with added complexity — transaction classification, mid-period capital contribution, opening GST balance. Already worked through in depth, so this is review.

### 3. Q5 — Maximum Mowing *(the accrual concept)*
**Time:** ~45 min
**Role:** Introduces the accrual concept (expenses recorded when **incurred**, not when paid). Covers the qualitative characteristic of **relevance** — high-yield for the exam.

### 4. Q8 — Bobby's Boats *(revenue recognition)*
**Time:** ~30 min
**Role:** Covers the revenue recognition principle — when is revenue earned vs received? All conceptual, no number-crunching.

## Suggested Order and Timing

| Order | Question | Time | Why this order |
|:-:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Q1 — Learn to Play Guitar | 30 min | Confidence builder, locks in the basic mechanics |
| 2 | Q3 — Cavalieri's | 30 min | Reviews what's already been done — reinforces and extends Q1 |
| 3 | Q5 — Maximum Mowing | 45 min | Step up into accrual concepts. Hardest of the four. |
| 4 | Q8 — Bobby's Boats | 30 min | Switch gears to conceptual writing — fresh skill, different muscle |

**Plus a 5-min skim of Q7's solution** at the end — same matching principle in a different context (prepaid rent), reinforces the depreciation concept already understood.

---

## Why This Works

- **Q1 first** gives a quick win. Right tone for the session.
- **Q3 second** builds directly on Q1 with extra layers, and the heavy lifting is already done.
- **Q5 third**, when he's warmed up but not yet exhausted, because it requires the most thinking.
- **Q8 last** is a different kind of task (writing, not calculating), so the mental switch acts as a kind of rest.

---

## What to Skip (and Why)

- **Q2 (Blyth Mechanical)** — same skills as Q1, just longer. Diminishing returns.
- **Q4 (Classic Photography)** — adds source-document reading, time-consuming, not high-yield for this exam.
- **Q6 (Tan the Painter)** — overlaps with Q5 and Q8. If those land, this is covered.
- **Q7 (Jetski Hire)** — read the solution only. Same matching principle as depreciation.

---

## How to Approach Each Question

For every question:

1. **Attempt it on his own first** — even if it feels slow. Struggle is where the learning happens.
2. **Check against the worked solution** — but focus on *where his thinking diverged*, not just whether the number matched.
3. **Say the reasoning out loud** — "I excluded the loan because it's a liability, not revenue." Verbalising locks it in.
4. **End each question with a one-sentence summary** of what it taught him.
