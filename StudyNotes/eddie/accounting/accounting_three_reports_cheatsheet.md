# The Three Accounting Reports — How They Connect

## What Each Report Tells You

| Report | Question it answers | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| **Income Statement** | Did the business make a profit? | For a period (e.g. month of January) |
| **Cash Flow Statement** | Where did the cash come from and go? | For a period (e.g. month of January) |
| **Balance Sheet** | What does the business own and owe right now? | At a point in time (e.g. as at 31 January) |

The first two are like **videos** — what happened over a period. The Balance Sheet is a **photograph** — the state of the business at one moment.

## How They Connect

The three reports are not a hierarchy — they are three lenses on the same business. Two key numbers flow into the Balance Sheet:

- **Income Statement → Net Profit → added to Owner's Equity**
- **Cash Flow Statement → Closing Bank Balance → appears under Current Assets**

## The Key Formulas

- **Income Statement:** Revenue earned − Expenses incurred = Net Profit (or Net Loss)
- **Owner's Equity:** Opening Capital + Capital Contributions + Net Profit − Drawings = Closing Capital
- **Balance Sheet identity:** Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity

## The Classic Exam Trap

> *"A business made a net loss of $25,000 but its bank balance increased by $13,000. Explain how this is possible."*

The three reports measure different things:

- **Income Statement** measures earnings on an accrual basis (revenue earned, expenses incurred)
- **Bank balance** reflects cash on a cash basis (money in, money out)
- A **loan, capital contribution, or sale of a non-current asset** boosts cash without being revenue
- **Depreciation** reduces profit without reducing cash
- **Drawings** reduce cash without affecting profit

A business can be **cash-positive and unprofitable** at the same time — or **profitable and cash-poor**. That's why you need all three reports.

## Why This Matters

If you can confidently explain that a business can make a profit while losing cash (or the reverse), you've understood the most important concept in Yr 11 Accounting.
